Aim Training Guide for Blood Strike

Updated June 27, 2026 · 15 min read
AD PLACEHOLDER — Banner (728x90)

If you want to rank up in Blood Strike, aim is the single most important skill to improve. This guide covers the exact drills, settings, and routines that pro players use to stay sharp.

1. Find Your Sensitivity

Most pro players use 400-800 DPI with in-game sensitivity between 1.5 and 3.0. Start here and adjust until you can do a full 180-degree turn with one comfortable swipe across your mousepad.

Quick test: Your crosshair should stay on a bot's head while you strafe left and right. If it bounces, your sens is too high.

2. Daily Aim Routine (15 Minutes)

  • Tracking (5 min): Strafe side to side in front of a bot, keeping your crosshair on its head. Do this 3 rounds.
  • Flick shots (5 min): Aim at one target, quickly flick to another. Focus on speed then accuracy.
  • Spray control (5 min): Learn the spray pattern of your main weapon. Practice the reverse pattern in the training range.

3. Crosshair Placement

Always keep your crosshair at head height. When rounding corners, pre-aim where enemies are likely to be. This eliminates the need to flick vertically and speeds up your reaction time by 200-300ms.

4. Recommended Settings

  • DPI: 800
  • In-game sens: 2.0-2.5
  • ADS sensitivity: 0.8-1.0
  • Scope sensitivity: 0.6-0.8
  • Raw input: ON
  • V-Sync: OFF

5. Advanced Techniques

Once you have the basics down, practice jump-peeking, shoulder-peeking, and counter-strafing. These advanced movement techniques let you gather information without exposing yourself to enemy fire.
